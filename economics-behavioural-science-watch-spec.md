# Economics & Behavioural Science Watch — Briefing Specification

## Purpose

A weekly long-form reading briefing covering economic science, behavioural science, and financial economics — sourced exclusively from academic literature (peer-reviewed journals and working papers with equal standing). Published every **Tuesday**. Primary purpose: personal enrichment and a content pipeline for 利世民頻道 and Unsubject. Connections to episode angles are implicit — not flagged explicitly in the briefing.

---

## Briefing Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Cadence | Weekly, every Tuesday |
| Length | ~2,000+ words per issue |
| Depth | Long-form deep read |
| Language | English only |
| Sources | Academic only — journals + working papers, equal standing |
| Citation format | Full citation per paper: authors, year, journal/venue, DOI |
| Paper distribution | 3–4 papers at equal depth (~400–500 words each) |
| Market/policy signals | Light touch — noted when present, not a dedicated section |
| Episode angles | Implicit only — reader draws own connections |

## Domain Scope

| Domain | What counts |
|---|---|
| Economic Science | Macroeconomics, microeconomics, institutional economics, political economy, development economics, labour, public finance |
| Behavioural Science | Behavioural economics, psychology of decision-making, cognitive biases, nudge theory, experimental economics, neuroeconomics |
| Financial Economics | Asset pricing, market microstructure, corporate finance, risk, derivatives, household finance, financial intermediation |

Cross-domain papers that bridge two or more of the above are prioritised — they tend to be the most generative for content.

## Issue Structure

**1. Opening Frame** (2–3 paragraphs)
What is the intellectual thread connecting this week's papers? This is not a summary — it is an analytical framing that earns the reader's time. Establish the tension, the open question, or the shift in thinking that makes this set of papers worth reading together.

**2. Paper Entries** (3–4 papers, ~400–500 words each)

Each paper entry follows this format:

> **Full Citation**
> Authors (Year). "Title." *Journal or Venue*. DOI or URL.

- **The question** — what problem or puzzle is this paper addressing?
- **The approach** — method, data, natural experiment, model, or framework used
- **The finding** — what they found, with appropriate epistemic humility about causality vs. correlation
- **Why it matters** — broader significance for the field, for understanding human behaviour, or for markets/policy
- **Market or policy note** *(light touch, only if genuinely present)* — one sentence if applicable; omit if not

**3. Cross-Paper Synthesis** (3–4 paragraphs)
What do these papers, read together, reveal that no single paper captures alone? This is the most valuable section — the connections, contradictions, open questions, and intellectual provocation that emerge from juxtaposition.

**4. Paper Log Updates** *(brief)*
New entries added to the Economics & Behavioural Science Paper Log this issue.

## Source Hierarchy

**Economics journals (top-tier, equal standing)**
American Economic Review (AER), Quarterly Journal of Economics (QJE), Journal of Political Economy (JPE), Review of Economic Studies (RES), Econometrica

**Finance journals (top-tier, equal standing)**
Journal of Finance (JF), Journal of Financial Economics (JFE), Review of Financial Studies (RFS), Review of Finance (RoF)

**Behavioural & psychology journals (top-tier, equal standing)**
Psychological Science, Journal of Economic Perspectives (JEP), Cognition, Journal of Experimental Psychology, Psychological Review

**Working paper repositories (equal standing with journals)**
NBER Working Papers, SSRN, CEPR Discussion Papers, Brookings Papers on Economic Activity, Federal Reserve working paper series

**Secondary sources** *(for context only — never as primary source)*
VoxEU/CEPR Policy Portal, IGM Forum, IMF Blog, NBER Digest

**Avoid:** non-peer-reviewed commentary, think-tank opinion pieces, journalism, op-eds — even from economists. The briefing is paper-first.

---

## Citation Format

Every paper featured in an issue receives a full citation block:

> **Authors, Initials. (Year). "Full Paper Title." *Journal Name*, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx**

For working papers:

> **Authors, Initials. (Year). "Full Paper Title." *NBER Working Paper No. XXXXX* / *SSRN Working Paper*. https://doi.org/xxxxx or URL**

---

## Market & Policy Signal Convention

When a paper has genuine market or policy implications, append a single sentence at the end of its entry:

*Market/policy note: [one sentence only — the specific implication and who it affects]*

Do not force this. Many papers will have no market or policy note. The convention exists to surface the ones that genuinely do.

## Paper Log Fields

The **Economics & Behavioural Science Paper Log** is a cumulative record of every paper featured across all issues. It serves as a personal reading archive and a searchable source database for future content work.

| Field | Options / Notes |
|---|---|
| Name | Paper title |
| Authors | Full author list |
| Year | Publication or working paper year |
| Journal or Venue | e.g. AER, QJE, NBER Working Paper, SSRN |
| Status | Peer-Reviewed / Working Paper / Forthcoming |
| Domain | Economic Science / Behavioural Science / Financial Economics / Cross-domain |
| DOI or URL | Canonical link to paper |
| Date Added | Date first featured in a briefing issue |
| Notes | Brief note on why this paper was selected |
