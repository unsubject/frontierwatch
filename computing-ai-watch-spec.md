# Computing & AI Watch — Briefing Specification

## Purpose

A weekly personal briefing covering AI/ML, quantum computing, and computer science theory — focused on developments that are physically meaningful, economically significant, or investment-relevant. Published every **Wednesday**. Distinct from Energy Watch; no cross-referencing unless an item is genuinely dual-domain (e.g. AI compute costs).

---

## Briefing Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Cadence | Weekly, every Wednesday |
| Length | ~1,000–1,500 words per issue |
| Depth | Mixed — some technical detail + so-what framing |
| Language | English only |
| Paper coverage | Landmark papers only — not every issue |
| Investment signals | Yes — flag companies, sectors, trends explicitly |

---

## Domain Scope

| Domain | What counts |
|---|---|
| AI & ML | Foundation models, training/inference advances, agents, multimodal, alignment milestones, benchmark shifts |
| Quantum Computing | Hardware milestones (qubit counts, error rates, coherence), algorithm breakthroughs, commercial deployments |
| CS Theory & Algorithms | Complexity results, new algorithmic paradigms, cryptography, formal verification, optimization |
| Hardware & Chips | AI accelerators, neuromorphic, photonics — only when directly enabling the above domains |

---

## Issue Structure

**1. Opening Frame** (1–2 paragraphs)
The overarching shift or tension this week. Sets the analytical lens for everything that follows.

**2. Main Items** (3–5 items, ~150–250 words each)

Each item uses this format:
- **What it is** — factual description of the development
- **Why it matters technically** — the mechanism or significance in one paragraph
- **So what** — economic or strategic implication; who benefits, who is threatened
- **Investment signal** *(if applicable)* — companies/sectors with ticker if public

**3. Landmark Paper Spotlight** *(conditional — only when genuinely field-shifting)*
- Paper title, authors, venue
- Core claim in plain language
- Why this one matters beyond the abstract

**4. Watchlist Updates** *(brief)*
New entries or status changes to the Computing & AI Frontier Watchlist.

**5. One Question to Sit With**
A single open question raised by this week's material — not answered, just posed. For reflection and potential episode angles.

---

## Source Hierarchy

1. **Primary research** — arXiv, Nature, Science, NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR/STOC/FOCS proceedings, ACM/IEEE
2. **Company technical blogs** — Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, IBM Quantum, Microsoft Research, IonQ
3. **Institutional reports** — NIST, DARPA, NSF
4. **Quality tech journalism** — MIT Technology Review, Ars Technica, The Gradient, Import AI (Jack Clark)
5. **Market/investment lens** — Bloomberg Intelligence, Semianalysis, Stratechery *(when relevant)*

**Avoid:** press releases without technical substance, pure benchmark marketing, hype-driven VC announcements without reproducible results.

---

## Investment Signal Framework

When flagging investment angles, structure as follows:

- **Sector theme** — e.g. "inference efficiency", "quantum error correction hardware"
- **Key players** — public companies (with ticker), private companies worth tracking, research labs
- **Stage** — Research / Early Commercial / Deployed
- **Risk note** — what would invalidate the thesis
